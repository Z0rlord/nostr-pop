# Start Here — Student Entry Path

Practical orientation for new **Tenshinryu Hyoho** students — whether you train at a Japan branch, through **Tenshinryu ONLINE / KIWAMI**, or are exploring before your first class.

## What this wiki is

This site is a structured knowledge base drawn from official Tenshinryu sources (tenshinryu.net, instructor texts, curriculum books). It is **not** a substitute for live instruction.

Advanced curriculum — especially [[techniques/tachiai-12-kata|twelve seihō]] and [[concepts/miden-kurai-no-koto|miden kurai]] — is **confidential teaching (門外不出)**: study under your instructor's guidance. Do not treat wiki pages as permission to teach outside the lineage.

For the big picture of how hubs connect, see [[synthesis|Synthesis]].

## Week 1 checklist

Work through these in order during your first week:

1. **Overview** — [[overview|What Tenshinryu Hyoho is]]; eight arts at a glance.
2. **Reiho basics** — etiquette before technique:
   - [[reiho/tabi|How to wear tabi]]
   - [[reiho/sageo|About the sageo]]
   - [[reiho/keiko-osame|Keiko osame (closing practice)]]
   - Full list: [[reiho/_index|Reiho index]]
3. **What a class feels like** — a typical session flow (opening bow, narashi-geiko, main themes, closing):

> **From instructor text (Vol. II):** Arrive early; greet students; open with **zarei** (seated bow), optional **ken-za-zen**, then **kinchō**. **Narashi-geiko** repeats draws without heavy correction (~10–20 reps). Main blocks include **doran-keiko**, instructional talk on bushidō and history, then standing **battojutsu** / **kenjutsu** themes. Close with remarks and **keiko osame**.

   Full detail: [[guides/instructor-3rd-grade/vol-2-training-flow|The Flow of Training]].
