# Tenshinryu Hyoho

**Tenshinryu** (天心流) is a traditional Japanese **hyoho** (兵法) — a comprehensive martial art system — tracing to the early Edo period (~400 years ago). It integrates sword, spear, chain weapons, and unarmed methods, with emphasis on tactics usable across everyday postures and environments.

See also: [[history/overview]] · [[arts/_index]]

## At a glance

| | |
|---|---|
| **Founder** | Tokizawa Yahei (時沢弥兵衛), under Yagyu Munenori |
| **Era** | Kan'ei period; formalized from Shinkageryu and allied traditions |
| **Purpose (historical)** | Training for **Kogan** (光願), covert warriors supporting Tokugawa peace |
| **Current head** | Lineage through Ishii Seizo (8th) → Nakamura Tenshin (9th) |
| **Modern reach** | Kanto dojo network; international chapters; [[TENSHINRYU ONLINE]] via KIWAMI |

## What makes it "hyoho"

Unlike a single-weapon specialty school, Tenshinryu lists eight major **arts** (see [[arts/_index]]). Training includes castle-context draw-and-cut methods (**denchu toho saya no uchi**), **koden** long-sword forms, and daily wear of **wakizashi** — reflecting samurai-ready assumptions.

## Learning today

- **In Japan**: multiple practice locations (Kanto and elsewhere) — details on official site.
- **International**: federation branches worldwide; online curriculum through [Tenshinryu KIWAMI](https://tenshinryu.xyz).
- **Core batto curriculum**: [[techniques/tachiai-12-kata]] — twelve standing seiho (勢法).
