# Kata Culture — Believe in Kata, Not Kata Alone

*2024-11-22* — Traditional culture

Part III of three:

1. [[articles/p-2584|On having many techniques]]
2. [[articles/p-2600|One mesh cannot catch a bird]]
3. **[[articles/p-2605|Believe in kata, yet do not believe in kata alone]]** (this page)

*Recap of Part I:* diverse **seihō** build minimum experience for varied situations — injury, terrain, equipment. Masters who say “we have no such technique” cannot respond when there is no redo. Tenshinryu, from Shinkage lineage, includes rich battojutsu — **108** draw variations from single-draw to transformed draws; seated with **okigatana**, daily **daishō**, **haiza** with both swords, **haiza** wearing **daitō** — preserving warrior dress is rare among koryū; one-sword-only iai schools criticize two-sword practice, but for Tenshinryu **training dress equals daily dress** — mismatch risks fatal hesitation (e.g. removing **wakizashi** before drawing when there is no time). **Naginata**, **yari**, **kusarigama**, **yawara** keiko also wear **daishō**; seated jujutsu wears **wakizashi** by principle. We honor preservation but do not demand all schools copy us — “sleeping samurai wore daishō?” No — Tenshinryu removes swords as occasion requires; sleep is **mu-koshi**. Changing one-sword tradition because outsiders misunderstand would betray generations.

Many techniques embody **crisis management** — learning responses for situations; some “cowardly” techniques have limited use; no technique is perfect; real moments break form — adapt, sometimes draw not, sometimes fight not — application needs ideal form first, but same moves only constrain freedom — hence many patterns in Tenshinryu. Warrior combat is not sword alone — **naginata**, **yari**, **kusarigama**, empty-hand **yawara**.

## Nature and classification of techniques

Especially in batto: facing already in combat; sudden defense; preemptive strike. Broad classes (author’s labels, not official Tenshinryu terms):

- **Taishi ken** — mutual combat (kenjutsu base)
- **Goshin-hō** (保身法) — sudden attack
- **Uchihatashi** — you attack (e.g. order to strike)

One type alone cannot cover a warrior’s life. Most Edo samurai never drew in life — yet preparedness matters. “Sleeping, rising, sitting, standing, walking, running” — techniques multiply for each; training experience still serves in crisis like evacuation drills — not meaningless if principle is sound.

Discarding all as “outdated” because we no longer wave swords is easy and wrong — moderns who never lived as bushi will mis-sort. Tenshinryu aims to transmit **everything** — else we betray Ishii Sensei who passed all though “times had changed.” **Sōden** means **full** transmission — not cutting canvas to “what modern people like.” Passing all under harsh modern conditions is arduous; many schools died in history’s waves — Tenshinryu barely spun a thin thread to us — unacceptable for our generation to cut it.

## Many vs few; multi-tier structure

Many techniques have necessity and advantage; few have advantage too — easy to memorize, fast learning. Hence **multi-tier structure**: ranks **shokyo, chūkyo, okkyo, kirigami, mokuroku, menkyo, sōden (kaiden)** — seven stages. *Shokyo–okkyo* from tea/flower arts — “deep entrance” before true inner training; batto historically from **ippon-nuki** point-draw without footwork, then motion, then **ō** formal **seihō** — today schedule forces teaching advanced material early so disciples meet Nakamura Sensei while he can still teach — stages collapsed intentionally.

## Yin voluntary muscles

Many **seihō** train not only technique acquisition but **multi-angle body use** — attention from Kōno Yoshinori, Kuroda Tetsuzan, Itō Noboru, Takaoka Hideo, functional anatomy. What seemed inefficient kata often demands **voluntary muscles** we rarely use — most muscle is voluntary; heart etc. are involuntary. Even “using the whole body,” many parts **slack** — adolescents slacking less; by high school, overworked vs slack muscles diverge — author calls slack voluntary muscle **yin voluntary muscle**. Wake it by **datsuryoku** — reducing overworked load; koryū pass datsuryoku methods; Tenshinryu’s shoulder-width stand lifted by two partners — tense body lifts easily, released body does not. Same movement limits muscle groups — varied **seihō** wake many yin muscles.

## Secondary merits

Variety is also **fun** — same-only bores; deep study never ends but not all endure — spice for lifelong practice; instant-learning efficiency favors few forms, long path favors repertoire.

**Authority** — Musashi’s critique of selling many techniques to impress beginners applies — unique techniques advertise schools and sometimes decide outcomes — but side effect only; growing techniques for profit or monopoly is unfortunate if historical — still inherit and polish, not discard.

## Standard benefits of kata

- Acquire technique — how to move self and enemy  
- **Conditioning** — optimal attack/defense under scenario; in fight, technique appears — not “use kata” consciously  
- **Combat method** — safe effective lines; safety/effect ratio shows school design  
- **Maai** — distance  
- **Timing** — too early read, too late fails  
- **Body building** — school-ideal physique  

All need **correct** kata — wrong distance or attack fails; forcing wrong form as “application” risks regression. Kata have **set conditions** — breaking them in practice then calling kata “unrealistic” often means partner grabbed wrong in jujutsu or cut wrong place in kenjutsu. Tenshinryu saying: do not **fit** into kata — yet without measure, no kata at all; those who never trained correctly dismiss kata.

## Conclusion

Logical-sounding critiques abound: kata hollow; few is “real”; schools add kata for students and prestige — not always true — do not lightly deny deep tradition; silence lets critics win; “those who understand will understand” fails to **raise** those who understand.

Tenshinryu is living hyōhō with deep pulse — we live or kill it; our duty is to live it. Not every disciple must agonize — bright, fun, serious keiko preserves the pulse. Knowing **why** many **seihō** matter deepens value.

We live in an age of long study — may koryū with surrounding culture be loved as lifelong learning.

Kata culture is a treasure chest handing many messages through form — yet kata training’s worth is undervalued, especially in bujutsu — partly because kata’s power is hard to **show**; experts can look plain in “real” fight; modern “real” is competition — gap between kata’s assumed combat and shiai.

Still, demonstrating skill cultivated through kata at undeniable level would counter dismissal. Tenshinryu keiko is not **only** seihō — free exchange exists — not kata absolutism.

**Not “only kata is absolute” but “kata is absolute”** — **believe in seihō (kata), yet do not believe in seihō alone.**

Through kata culture we touch the school as metaphysical existence. Current kata may not be identical to founder’s — generations refined and added — yet that history **is** the school. To know, learn, taste — is kata training.

Japanese fixation on manual-like kata draws mockery — real fault of form — yet form is not useless. Schools exist complete against rigidity of body-mind; we must make that fact visible and blow away kata-dismissal.
