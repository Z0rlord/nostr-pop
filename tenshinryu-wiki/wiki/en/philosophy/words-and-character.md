# Let&#8217;s be careful. Our words build our character. (言葉と人格)

Let’s be careful. Our words build our character.│JAPANESE TRADITION TENSHINRYU HYOHO
Let’s be careful. Our words build our character.

Let’s be careful. Our words build our character.
When you vomit from your mouth, all but the closest people will avoid you. People who spend all their time talking badly about others are the same ones who vomit all the time. The only people who will come close to such a person are those who like to gossip and blindly believe in false and relied upon information. We know their lies. We know why they do what they do. So our hearts are quiet. The people who slander us have unclean minds, covered in the stench of their own vomit every day. So we stay out of it and keep our distance. Their mouths stink and their bodies are contaminated. They throw their filth, but it doesn’t reach us because we keep our distance. They don’t realize that their deeds are making their mouths, bodies, and hands dirty. They destroy themselves by their own actions. We are with our people every day to pass on the traditions properly and to pass them on. And we use our words to help newcomers who have not yet met us.
