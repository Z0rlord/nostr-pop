# Sojutsu (槍術)

The art of the **straight spear** (*suyari*, 素槍). In Tenshinryu hyoho it is one of eight major arts (see [[arts/_index]]) and part of the school's **long-weapon** training alongside cross-spear and glaive methods.

## Lineage note

The official site states that long-weapon arts — **sojutsu**, [[arts/jumonjiyarijutsu|jumonjiyarijutsu]], and [[arts/naginatajutsu|naginatajutsu]] — derive from **Hozoin-ryu In-ha** (宝蔵院分流陰派槍術), the spear tradition associated with Hozoin temple. Tenshinryu preserves these within its comprehensive hyoho rather than as a separate spear-only school.

## Core vs supplementary (tonomono)

Tenshinryu positions **denchu toho saya no uchi**, seated and standing batto, and **kenjutsu** as the center of training. **Sojutsu** is taught as **tonomono** (外物) or **heiden** (併伝, parallel transmission): offered according to the student's stage of practice and interest, not as an entry specialization.

The school does **not** accept students who seek only one supplementary art — comprehensive training is required for proper transmission (see [[arts/_index]] and official site policy on 外物).

## Samurai equipment context

Unlike many schools that train long weapons with sword removed, Tenshinryu practitioners wear **both long and short swords** during sojutsu (and other long-weapon) practice. The rationale: daily samurai equipment must not differ from training equipment, so draw and footwork are not obstructed in a sudden encounter (see [[philosophy/kata-culture]]).

## Combat role

Spear work addresses ranges and angles sword alone cannot cover. In the hyoho philosophy, samurai prepared for combat beyond the sword — **suyari**, cross-spear, naginata, chain weapons, and unarmed methods form a single crisis-management toolkit (see [[philosophy/kata-culture]]).

Advanced applications appear in castle-context and **shō no kurai** (将の位) material — seated techniques on a folding camp stool that apply batto, naginata, and spear principles for high-rank defenders (referenced on tenshinryu.net).
