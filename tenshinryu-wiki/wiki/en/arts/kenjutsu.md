# Kenjutsu (剣術)

The art of **sword combat with the blade already drawn** — paired and standing exchange, as distinct from [[arts/battojutsu|battojutsu]] (drawing and first cut). In Tenshinryu hyoho, kenjutsu is one of eight major arts and part of the **core curriculum** alongside seated and standing draw methods (see [[arts/_index]]).

## Role in Tenshinryu

The official Japanese site describes the school's center of gravity as **denchu toho saya no uchi**, seated and standing **battojutsu** with long and short sword, and **kenjutsu**. Other weapons are classified as **tonomono** (外物, supplementary transmission) and taught according to stage and student interest — but kenjutsu remains foundational sword work once both parties are in contact range.

Historically, Nakamura Shike's early teaching period (before retirement from postal work) emphasized battojutsu for lack of time; kenjutsu received even less regular instruction. After venues and teaching hours expanded, the full repertoire — including kenjutsu — could be transmitted systematically again (see [[philosophy/kata-culture]]).

## Classification of sword methods

Tenshinryu groups sword-related techniques by situation (see [[philosophy/kata-culture]]):

| Category | Japanese | Meaning |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Standoff | 対峙剣 *taiji-ken* | Both in combat-ready stances; basis of kenjutsu |
| Self-defense | 保躰法 *hoshinhō* | Response to sudden attack |
| Preemptive strike | 討ち太刀 *uchihatachi* | Initiating attack (e.g. on orders) |

**Taiji-ken** is the kenjutsu line proper: mutual readiness, maai (distance), and exchange with drawn swords.

## Body and lineage context

Standing posture in Tenshinryu draws on multiple kenjutsu layers preserved in **koden** (古伝):

- **Kaisha-kenjutsu** (介者剣術) — armored battlefield sword; reflected in [[concepts/kurai/robokudachi|Robokudachi]] and related kurai
- **Suhada-kenjutsu** (素肌剣術) — unarmored Edo-period exchange; reflected in [[concepts/kurai/shabokudachi|Shabokudachi]]

Practitioners train in **two-sword configuration** (daitō + wakizashi) even when the long sword is the primary weapon — a design choice rare among iai-focused schools but consistent with samurai daily wear (see [[arts/battojutsu]] and [[philosophy/kata-culture]]).

## Training equipment

Kenjutsu practice commonly uses the **fukuro-jinai** (袋竹刀), called **marudachi** (丸太刀) in Tenshinryu — bamboo covered in leather, lineage-linked to Shinkageryu and ancestral to the modern kendo shinai. See [[techniques/fukuro-jinai]] for construction, lacquer tradition, and dojo use.

## Relationship to other arts

- **Battojutsu** — draw and first cut; often precedes kenjutsu in a encounter
- **Koden tachi** — long-sword forms alongside uchigatana; not kenjutsu-only but shared sword principles
- **Yawara** — unarmed methods when the sword cannot be used; complementary crisis-management training
