# Kusarigamajutsu (鎖鎌術)

The art of the **chain-sickle** (*kusarigama*) — a weighted chain attached to a hand-held sickle or blade. Listed among eight major arts on the international site; in Japanese curricular terms it is **tonomono** (外物, supplementary transmission) alongside other non-core weapons (see [[arts/_index]]).

## Role in hyoho

Kusarigama belongs to the category of **irregular or specialized weapons** (*henzoku buki*, 変則武器) that Edo-period warriors might encounter. Tenshinryu's comprehensive philosophy holds that sword skill alone is insufficient for every battlefield or street encounter — chain weapons, spears, and unarmed methods fill the gap (see [[philosophy/kata-culture]]).

## Training conventions

As with [[arts/naginatajutsu|naginatajutsu]] and [[arts/sojutsu|sojutsu]], kusarigama practice is performed while wearing **both swords** (大小帯刀). The wakizashi must not be an afterthought: it can obstruct movement if the body has not trained with it present from the start.

## Transmission

Kusarigamajutsu is taught according to **stage of practice and student interest**, not as a standalone entry path. The school requires integrated hyoho study — see official policy on 外物 in [[arts/_index]] (from tenshinryu.net page 15).

## Distinction from tesajutsu

**[[arts/tesajutsu|Tesajutsu]]** (鉄鎖術) covers **iron chain** methods without the sickle component. Both appear in the eight-art list; they address overlapping but distinct weapon geometries.
