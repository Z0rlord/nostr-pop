# Battojutsu (抜刀術)

The art of **drawing and cutting** with the sword — from scabbard to first decisive action. In Tenshinryu hyoho it is one of eight major arts and, with **kenjutsu**, forms the most publicly documented sword curriculum (see [[arts/_index]]).

## Centrality in modern transmission

Battojutsu received the greatest teaching emphasis during periods when practice time was limited (notably Nakamura Shike's postal-career years). The twelve [[techniques/tachiai-12-kata|tachiai seiho]] became the flagship standing syllabus. After teaching expanded, batto remained the gateway to broader hyoho (see [[philosophy/kata-culture]]).

Site and book sources describe on the order of **108** draw-and-cut methods — from standard to unconventional — reflecting situational breadth rather than a single draw style.

## Major lines

### Denchu toho saya no uchi (殿中刀法鞘之中)

**Castle-context** sword methods — resolving conflict **inside the scabbard** where possible. Beginners receive this as foundational **saya no uchi** (鞘の中) principle and seiho from seated sword placement. See [[arts/_index]] special curricula.

### Za-hō (坐法)

**Seated** draw methods — long sword placed nearby (*okigatana*), **kodachi** (short sword) batto, cross-legged (*kōza* 胡坐) wear, and **haiza-hō** (廃坐法) with long sword worn while seated. Contrasts with schools that train iai with long sword only and no wakizashi.

### Tachiai battojutsu (立相抜刀術)

**Standing confrontation** — both parties facing for a duel. The twelve [[concepts/seiho|seiho]] of [[techniques/tachiai-12-kata]] are the advanced documented line. See [[concepts/tachiai]].

### Koden tachi

**Old transmission** long-sword (*tachi*) forms — including draw from **kōza** with tachi worn, not only **uchigatana** (打刀). See [[arts/_index]] koden note.

## Situation taxonomy

Batto methods map to combat phases (see [[philosophy/kata-culture]]):

| Type | Japanese | Role |
|------|----------|------|
| Standoff | 対峙剣 *taiji-ken* | Mutual readiness; overlaps [[arts/kenjutsu]] |
| Self-defense | 保躰法 *hoshinhō* | Sudden attack |
| Preemptive | 討ち太刀 *uchihatachi* | Striking on order or initiative |

## Equipment philosophy

Training assumes **two swords** when standing and **wakizashi** when seated with long sword placed aside — mirroring Edo samurai dress. Wakizashi is not removed for convenience; obstruction during draw is itself a training problem (see [[philosophy/kata-culture]]).

## Postural breadth

Official material cites tactics for **lying down, waking, sitting, standing, walking, and running**. [[techniques/nedachi|Nedachi]] (寝太刀) covers draw from recumbent position — one example of non-standing batto.
