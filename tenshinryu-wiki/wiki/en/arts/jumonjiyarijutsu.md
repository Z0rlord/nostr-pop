# Jumonjiyarijutsu (十文字槍術)

The art of the **cross-shaped spear** (*jūmonji-yari*, 十文字鑓 / 十文字槍). Distinguished from straight [[arts/sojutsu|sojutsu]] by the cruciform blade structure, it is one of eight major arts in Tenshinryu hyoho (see [[arts/_index]]).

## Hozoin connection

Like [[arts/sojutsu|sojutsu]] and [[arts/naginatajutsu|naginatajutsu]], jumonjiyarijutsu in Tenshinryu traces to **Hozoin-ryu In-ha** (宝蔵院分流陰派槍術). The Hozoin spear line is famous for cross-spear doctrine; Tenshinryu blog material references the Asari-family manuscript *Hozoin Jumonji Yari Yurushi Maki Ni* (宝蔵院十文字鑓許巻二) in discussions of kata culture (see [[philosophy/kata-culture]]).

## Curriculum placement

Cross-spear is classified as **tonomono** (外物) — supplementary to the core of **denchu toho**, battojutsu, and kenjutsu. Transmission depends on the practitioner's level and goals; the school requires **comprehensive** study rather than single-weapon specialization.

## Tactical role

The cross-spear excels at trapping, binding, and controlling an opponent's weapon at longer maai. Within hyoho, it complements straight spear and sword methods so a practitioner is not limited to one long-weapon geometry.

Training follows the same **two-sword wearing** rule as other long-weapon arts in Tenshinryu (see [[arts/sojutsu]] and [[philosophy/kata-culture]]).
