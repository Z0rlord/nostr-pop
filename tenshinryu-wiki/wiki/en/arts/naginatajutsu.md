# Naginatajutsu (薙刀術)

The art of the **glaive** (*naginata*) — a curved blade on a long shaft. One of eight major arts in Tenshinryu hyoho (see [[arts/_index]]), grouped with other **long weapons** under Hozoin-line spear tradition.

## Lineage

The official site notes that **sojutsu**, **jumonjiyarijutsu**, and **naginatajutsu** derive from **Hozoin-ryu In-ha** (宝蔵院分流陰派槍術). Naginata methods are preserved inside comprehensive hyoho rather than as a standalone naginata school.

## Core vs tonomono

Naginatajutsu is **tonomono** (外物) / **heiden** (併伝): taught when the student has sufficient foundation in core sword work and according to interest. Entry focused only on naginata (or any single supplementary art) is not accepted — see [[arts/_index]].

## Equipment and training design

Practitioners train naginata while wearing **daitō and wakizashi**, matching Edo-period samurai readiness. This is deliberate: a short sword at the hip affects stance, hip rotation, and emergency draw — conditions that must be rehearsed, not simulated only after class (see [[philosophy/kata-culture]]).

## Advanced context: shō no kurai

Castle and camp-stool scenarios (*shōgi* / 将几, **shō no kurai** 将の位) apply naginata (and batto, spear) principles for a seated commander under attack — described on tenshinryu.net as an aspect of **jōshi no ken** (城士の剣, the castle samurai's sword). Details are advanced transmission; the art page records the relationship only.
