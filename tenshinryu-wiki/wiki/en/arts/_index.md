## Core vs supplementary (tonomono)

The Japanese official site ([tenshinryu.net page 15](https://tenshinryu.net/?page_id=15)) distinguishes:

**Core (中心):** **Denchu toho saya no uchi**, seated and standing batto with long and short sword, and **kenjutsu**.

**Tonomono / heiden (外物・併伝):** Jujutsu ([[arts/yawara|yawara]]), kusarigama, straight spear, cross-spear, naginata — taught by **stage and student interest**. Entry seeking only one supplementary art is not accepted; comprehensive training is required.

Long weapons (sojutsu, jumonjiyarijutsu, naginatajutsu) trace to **Hozoin-ryu In-ha** (宝蔵院分流陰派槍術).

## Special curricula

- **Denchu Toho Saya no Uchi** (殿中刀法鞘之中) — sword methods for inside the castle; scabbard-centric context
- **Koden** (古伝) — old transmission; includes **tachi** (太刀) forms, not only **uchigatana** (打刀)
- **Wakizashi** (脇差) — worn in training as part of daily samurai equipment

## Training scope

Techniques and tactics for lying down, waking, sitting, standing, walking, and running — situational breadth beyond formal kata alone.
