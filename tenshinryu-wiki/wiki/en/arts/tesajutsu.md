# Tesajutsu (鉄鎖術)

The art of the **iron chain** (*tesa*, 鉄鎖) — flexible metal links used to strike, entangle, or disarm. One of eight arts named on the international Tenshinryu site (see [[arts/_index]]), distinct from [[arts/kusarigamajutsu|kusarigamajutsu]] (chain **and** sickle).

## Place in the curriculum

Published site material lists tesajutsu alongside kusarigama, spear, and glaive arts as components of **comprehensive hyoho**. Detailed kata names and progression are not yet published in this wiki's ingested sources; instruction belongs to licensed teachers.

Like other non-sword specialties, tesajutsu fits Tenshinryu's **crisis-management** aim: prepare for weapons and ranges outside standard sword doctrine (see [[philosophy/kata-culture]]).

## Relationship to kusarigama

| Art | Weapon | Emphasis |
|-----|--------|----------|
| [[arts/kusarigamajutsu]] | Chain + sickle | Entangle and cut at medium range |
| **Tesajutsu** | Iron chain alone | Binding, striking, control without blade on chain end |

Both are classified with **tonomono** (外物) supplementary material in Japanese curricular descriptions (see [[arts/kusarigamajutsu]]).

## Historical context

Chain arts flourished in the Edo period as concealed or secondary weapons. Tenshinryu preserves them within a multi-weapon system rather than as a separate chain-only ryu — consistent with the school's resistance to late-era **specialization** (sword-only, iai-only schools) described on tenshinryu.net.
