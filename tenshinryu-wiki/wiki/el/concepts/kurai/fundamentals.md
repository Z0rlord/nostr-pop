# Kurai fundamentals

## Ten · Shin · Chi (天・真・地)

- Height classification by fist/sword position:
- | Tier | Name | Common alias | Fist height |
- |------|------|--------------|-------------|
- | **Ten** | Ten-no-Kurai | Jodan 上段 | Above chest |
- | **Shin** | Shin-no-Kurai | Chudan 中段 | Torso level |
- | **Chi** | Chi-no-Kurai | Gedan 下段 | Below chest |
- Tip direction (**kissaki**) can also imply Ten/Shin/Chi — not height alone.

## In-Yo · Nakazumi · Mei-An

- **In (陰)** / **Yo (陽)** — right/left of **Nakazumi (中墨)** centerline
- **Mei-An (明暗)** — front and back
- Many kurai exist in mirrored In/Yo forms; **Myote (妙手)** hand switch possible

## Semeashi · Mamoriashi

| Foot forward | Name | Role |
|--------------|------|------|
| **Left** | Semeashi 攻め足 | Attack foot — pull left back → defense; step right forward → attack |
| **Right** | Mamoriashi 守り足 | Defense foot |

Semeashi is central to **Kojin (交刃)** — crossed-blade engagement.

## Γωνίες πίσω ποδιού (οδηγός)

| Name | Angle | Image |
|------|-------|-------|
| **Kagiashi** 鈎足 | ~45° | Hearth hook |
| **Chojiashi** 丁字足 | ~90° | 丁 shape to enemy |
| **Nenoashi** 根ノ足 | ~120° | Tree roots |

Body size and terrain vary — angles are optimization guides, not rigid rules.

## Παραλλαγές (μη εξαντλητικές)

- Foot switch; **Sanmi** stances; **Tendachi** (Musubi, Hesoku); **Shumokuashi** 撞木足; **Bajyodachi** 馬上立; In/Yo pairs; countless combinations — learn basics here, refine under instruction.
