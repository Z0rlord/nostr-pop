# What Is Tenshinryu!?

> Clipped from the official international site homepage. Formatting simplified; links preserved where noted.

## History (summary)

Tenshinryu is a traditional Japanese martial art originating ~400 years ago in the early Edo period. **Tokizawa Yahei**, who served **Yagyu Munenori**, developed the style under Munenori's orders.

Yagyu Munenori was an **Ometsuke** (inspector monitoring feudal lords and the Imperial Court) of the Tokugawa shogunate. Tenshinryu was devised for the **Kogan** (光願) — warriors (Shirindan 士林団) supporting the Tokugawa shogunate from the shadows. *Kogan* means "wish for light" — praying for continued peace.

Tokizawa Yahei organized wisdom and techniques from various schools and covert practitioners, based on the Yagyu family's **Shinkageryu**, and named it Tenshinryu.

The work was lost as peace prevailed; dissolved during the 8th shogun Yoshimune's reign. Some styles were publicized in Kansai and taught at clan schools (that line ceased). Some Kogan samurai preserved Tenshinryu for when peace was disturbed again.

Descendants of Kogan **kumigashira** Ishitsunemon preserved Tenshinryu in Kanto. **8th generation Ishii Seizo** succeeded and passed it to **9th generation Nakamura Tenshin**.

Founder: **Yahei Tokizawa (時沢弥兵衛)**. Born in Edo era **Kan'ei (寛永)**.

## Comprehensive martial arts (Hyoho)

Tenshinryu is a school of **Hyoho (兵法)** comprising:

- **Kenjutsu** (剣術) — sword
- **Battojutsu** (抜刀術) — drawing the sword
- **Sojutsu** (槍術) — spear
- **Jumonjiyarijutsu** (十文字槍術) — cross-shaped spear
- **Naginatajutsu** (薙刀術) — glaive
- **Kusarigamajutsu** (鎖鎌術) — chain sickle
- **Tesajyutsu** (鉄鎖術) — chain
- **Yawara** (柔) — unarmed grappling

Additional study:

- **Denchu Toho Saya no Uchi** (殿中刀法鞘之中) — techniques for inside the castle
- **Koden** (古伝) — old styles including **Tachi** (太刀), not only **Uchigatana** (打刀)
- Training with **Wakizashi** (脇差) worn, reflecting samurai lifestyle
- Tactics for lying down, waking, sitting, standing, walking, running

## Modern organization

~15 years ago **Kuwami Shike** introduced Tenshinryu widely; growth from <5 students to multiple Kanto training centers.

**Ide Shike** (international division CEO) built worldwide chapters and practice groups.
